## Input

Summarize the known risks of a small-cap equity allocation.

## Output

### Response

<pre>A small-cap equity allocation can add diversification relative to large-cap stocks, but it carries several well-established risks:

- **Higher price volatility and drawdowns.** Small-cap stocks have historically tended to exhibit greater return variability than large-cap stocks. Their prices can move sharply when economic growth slows, financing conditions tighten, or investor risk appetite declines.

- **Greater business and financial fragility.** Smaller companies often have narrower product lines, less diversified revenue, weaker bargaining power, and fewer financial resources than large firms. A lost customer, supply disruption, recession, or refinancing need can therefore have a disproportionately large effect.

- **Liquidity and transaction-cost risk.** Many small-cap shares trade less frequently and in lower volumes. This can create wider bid—ask spreads, greater market impact when buying or selling, and difficulty exiting positions during stressed markets. The risk is especially relevant for actively managed strategies and very large funds relative to the capacity of the small-cap market.

- **Financing and interest-rate sensitivity.** Small companies can be more dependent on bank lending or capital markets to fund operations and growth. Higher interest rates or restricted credit availability may raise their costs of capital or limit access to financing.

- **Less information and potentially weaker governance.** Analyst coverage, disclosure resources, and institutional scrutiny are often more limited for smaller companies. This can increase uncertainty about earnings quality, management execution, and corporate governance. It can also make valuations more prone to abrupt reassessment when new information arrives.

- **Concentration risk within small-cap benchmarks.** Broad small-cap indexes hold many securities, reducing single-company risk, but they may still have meaningful exposures to particular sectors, economically sensitive firms, low-profitability companies, or —micro-cap— names depending on the index definition. Small-cap allocations are not interchangeable: Russell 2000, S&amp;P SmallCap 600, and international small-cap indexes use different eligibility and profitability criteria.

- **Long periods of relative underperformance.** The historical —small-cap premium— is not dependable over any particular horizon. Small caps have experienced extended periods of lagging large caps, including in the U.S. during much of the 2010s and early 2020s. A small-cap allocation can therefore create substantial tracking error versus a large-cap-heavy benchmark.

- **Style exposure may drive results more than size alone.** Research has found that some of the apparent historical return advantage of small stocks is closely connected to other characteristics, especially value, profitability, quality, and investment patterns. A small-cap fund that owns financially weak or expensive companies may behave quite differently from a profitable or value-oriented small-cap strategy.

- **Index implementation and reconstitution effects.** Small-cap indexes must regularly add, delete, and rebalance holdings as firms grow, shrink, merge, or become ineligible. Trading around those events can impose costs and create performance differences between an index and a fund tracking it.

- **International and emerging-market additions add separate risks.** A global small-cap allocation can improve diversification but introduces currency, country, political, market-access, and settlement risks in addition to the usual small-company risks.

Diversification through a broad small-cap fund can reduce company-specific risk, but it does not remove the systematic risks of equity-market declines, illiquidity, or prolonged relative underperformance.

   https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1540-6261.1992.tb04398.x

4. S&amp;P Dow Jones Indices, *S&amp;P U.S. Indices Methodology* — details eligibility, liquidity, financial-viability, and rebalancing rules relevant to small-cap index construction.  
   https://www.spglobal.com/spdji/en/documents/methodologies/methodology-sp-us-indices.pdf
